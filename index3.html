Introduction

Water Wastage in domestic and agriculture sector

Water is a resource that supports life on our planet. Its mishandling and Wasting, has continously increasing in both households and agriculture. The challenges associated with water wastage have a history. In the past, mismanagement of water resources often developed from a lack of understanding about the nature of this resource. Many households were unaware of their water consumption levels while agricultural practices heavily depend on irrigation methods. However as populations grew and environmental awareness increased, the issue of water wastage be relevant. Today the problem of water wastage remains a concern in settings. Numerous households still employ fixtures and appliances that consume amounts of water. Additionally everyday activities such as overwatering grassland, leaving taps running unnecessarily and neglecting to repair leaks contribute to this issue. In the sector, water wastage causes a threat to global food security. Traditional irrigation methods that are widely used result in loss through evaporation, overflow and unnecessarily distribution. Expanding farming into water-rich areas can causes environmental issues.
